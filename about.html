About

Data was never the plan — it's just where the questions led.

I started in human physiology, then neuroscience — trying to understand how systems process signals and adapt. Data and analytics engineering turned out to be the same problem at a different scale: take noisy signals from a business, build the platform that makes sense of them, and turn that into decisions people actually act on. Six years on, I've done that across SaaS, proptech, e-commerce and sport — building teams and platforms from the ground up, and shipping AI-powered products (summarisation, forecasting, pricing intelligence) that move real commercial numbers.

Outside of work, I play guitar — it's the one thing I do that has nothing to do with a dashboard.
